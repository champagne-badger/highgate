Where to Eat & Drink in Highgate N6

A Local Guide

```

Nestled in leafy North London, Highgate N6 offers a food and drink scene that perfectly matches its village charm. From historic gastropubs frequented by literary legends to cozy cafes tucked into scenic parks, this guide covers the best spots to eat and drink in one of London's most atmospheric neighborhoods.

Whether you're looking for a Sunday roast after a walk on Hampstead Heath, a specialty coffee in a peaceful setting, or simply the perfect pint in a historic pub, Highgate has something special waiting for you.

Award-Winning Gastropubs

The Red Lion & Sun
Top 10 UK Gastropub • 25 North Road, N6 4BE

Consistently ranked among Britain's best gastropubs, The Red Lion & Sun has been serving Highgate since the 16th century. This family-run establishment earned the 2018 Great British Pub Awards and maintains a spot in the national Top 10.

What sets it apart: A daily changing menu posted online by 11am, featuring rustic modern British cuisine with ingredients from premium suppliers including Natoora, Rossmore Oysters, and Highgate Butchers. The wine list is widely considered one of London's finest.

Perfect for: Sunday roasts (arrive early as tables disappear quickly), seafood and oysters, or settling in by the log burner with an exceptional bottle from their curated collection. Two beer gardens provide lovely outdoor options.

The Bull
Gastropub with Microbrewery • 13 North Hill, N6

A lively neighborhood gastropub with a huge leafy terrace that's perfect for sunny afternoons. The Bull brings countryside charm to Highgate with dried hops decorating the interior and Labradors welcome on leashes.

Special feature: On-site microbrewery (London Brewing Company) offering 21 taps of cellar-chilled craft beers and seasonal ales. Thursday steak nights and daily pie specials enhance the traditional gastropub menu.

Happy hour highlight: Fridays 5-6pm with buy-one-get-one-free on Asahi, Lucky Saint, house wine and Prosecco.

The Flask
Historic Fuller's Pub • 77 Highgate West Hill, N6 6BU

A North London institution with centuries of history, The Flask has welcomed famous authors, playwrights, and rock stars through its doors. This Fuller's pub combines heritage with quality food and drink.

The setting features refined pub classics made from fresh seasonal ingredients, served in cozy historic interiors or outside seating areas. A real fire adds warmth during colder months.

Currently offering: Prix fixe menu throughout January, plus an extensive selection ranging from modern craft beers to traditional cask ales.

Traditional Pubs & Bars

The Wrestlers
Traditional English Pub • 98 North Road, N6 4AA

Standing on its present site since 1547, The Wrestlers is a hidden gem just two minutes from Highgate Village. The current owners have been running this traditional hostelry since 1997, maintaining its authentic character.

Features original fireplace, wooden furniture, and stained glass alongside a charming beer garden seating up to 40. Sunday roasts include lamb, pork, beef, chicken, plus vegetarian Wellington and vegan nut roast options, all served with seasonal vegetables, roast potatoes, homemade gravy, and Yorkshire pudding.

The Duke's Head
Country Music Honky Tonk • 16 Highgate High Street, N6 5JG

Since 2020, North London's only country music-themed American-style dive bar. Walls covered in 1970s and 80s music memorabilia create an authentic Nashville atmosphere without the transatlantic flight.

Offering over 100 different bourbons, whiskeys and ryes, plus PBR on draught and American beer-and-bourbon-shot deals. Live music, regular country karaoke, and hearty American fare including wings, brisket, and mac and cheese from Rack City Ribs.

The Boogaloo
Rock 'n' Roll Bar • Archway Road

A legendary live music venue that's welcomed Coldplay and Pete Doherty among others. Famous for its award-winning eclectic jukebox, with musical legends including Iggy Pop, Ronnie Wood, The Pogues, and The Libertines contributing their favorite albums to the playlist.

Grab a table in the snug den for juicy burgers and indie tunes from the vintage jukebox. The perfect spot for music lovers seeking an authentic North London experience.

The Gatehouse
Spanish Tapas Pub with Theatre • 1 North Road, N6

An imposing black-and-white pub in central Highgate offering enjoyable tapas alongside traditional pub fare and a great selection of beers. Features a sun-drenched beer garden for summer days and a cozy interior with comfy armchairs and a real fire for winter.

Unique feature: Houses one of London's leading off-West End theatres upstairs, making it ideal for pre- or post-show drinks and dining.

The Angel Inn
Contemporary Gastropub • 37 Highgate High Street, N6 5JT

Vibrant interiors meet hearty grub and an impressive array of craft pints. Popular for after-work drinks, Sunday roasts, and weekend bottomless brunches in a relaxed, welcoming atmosphere.

Coffee Shops & Cafes

The Pavilion Cafe
Park Cafe • Waterlow Park

Sitting right inside Highgate's Waterlow Park, this scenic cafe offers a quiet, light-filled space perfect for post-walk refreshments. Popular with locals, families, dog walkers, and runners stopping for coffee.

The menu is unfussy but carefully prepared: porridge, avocado and feta toast, proper coffee. Must try: Avocado Tomato on Toast with Poached Egg. After your meal, the rest of the beautiful park awaits exploration.

Monmouth Coffee
Specialty Coffee • 31 North Road, N6 4BE

The renowned coffee roasters bring their expertise to Highgate with high-quality beans and expert preparation. A favorite for coffee enthusiasts seeking the perfect brew in a relaxed setting.

Caffe Nero
Chain Coffee Shop • 62 Highgate High Street, N6 5HX

Reliable Italian-style coffee and pastries in the heart of Highgate Village. Convenient for a quick coffee while exploring the high street.

High Tea of Highgate
Tea Shop • 50 Highgate High Street, N6 5HX

A beautiful tea shop filled with adorable tea selections and traditional afternoon tea service. Perfect for a quintessentially British tea experience in charming surroundings.

Queen's Wood Cafe
Hidden Woods Cafe • Queen's Wood

Tucked away in a corner of the woods, this cafe occupies a restored 1898 wood keeper's lodge. Serves wholesome, homemade food made with seasonal produce from their community garden.

The tranquil atmosphere and varied cultural events make it ideal for escaping London's hustle. A uniquely peaceful setting for a soothing cup of coffee surrounded by nature.

Local Insider Tip

For the best coffee shop experience, visit Vanilla on Jacksons Lane (269a Archway Road). Owner Anna creates exceptional homemade breads that locals rave about. The beetroot juice and carrot cake are legendary. Arrive outside peak lunch hours for the best chance of getting a table in this neighborhood gem.

Restaurants & Eateries

Archie's
All-Day Eatery • Near Archway

A buzzy neighborhood eatery serving food all day while also selling houseplants. This sunny spot with indoor and outdoor tables is especially popular for brunch.

Menu features tacos and salads at lunch, sophisticated global plates in the evening, plus excellent coffee and cocktails throughout the day. The relaxed, welcoming vibe makes it easy to linger.

Turkuaz Brasserie
Turkish-Mediterranean • Highgate

Brings authentic Turkish flavors to Highgate with patterned tiles, sunny interiors, and warm, easygoing atmosphere. Classic Mediterranean menu with standout brunch offerings.

Must try: Menemen (soft scrambled eggs with tomatoes and peppers). The full Mediterranean breakfast is excellent for heartier appetites.

Bistro Laz
Turkish-Italian Fusion • Near Highgate High Street

Unique neighborhood eatery blending Italian and Turkish cuisine in a laid-back setting decorated with colorful traditional lamps and rustic exposed brick.

Extensive menu includes wraps, mezze, crepes, pancakes, and pizza. Casual and unfussy, perfect for a relaxed meal with coffee or cocktails.

Cool Carrot
New York Meets Sri Lanka • Highgate

An unexpected fusion bringing together New York comfort food, Sri Lankan spice, and London cafe culture. The menu skips typical brunch formulas for something more creative.

Highlights include proper bagels, warming Sri Lankan curries, and generous cake slices. Smoothies like Melon Refresher and Avo Gogo are particularly good. Somehow, it all works beautifully.

Kiplings
Indian Restaurant • Highgate

Popular locally for Indian meals and takeaways in an intimate, traditional setting. Specializes in Bengali and Bangladeshi dishes with modern interpretations of classic recipes.

Broad menu features extensive curry selection alongside other regional specialties, ensuring something for everyone.

Lure Fish Kitchen
Seafood Restaurant • Highgate

Seafood-focused dining featuring traditional fish and chips alongside inventive seasonal recipes. Modern, trendy dining room combines cool blue tones with earthy colors and materials.

Options include steamed mussels with various seasonings, plus dedicated children's dishes. Fresh, quality seafood in a contemporary setting.

Pizza Express
Italian Chain • Highgate High Street

Reliable pizza and pasta in a convenient location. Good for families and casual dining in the heart of Highgate Village.

Planning Your Visit to Highgate

Getting There: Highgate is served by Highgate tube station (Northern Line). The village is also accessible via bus routes and is approximately 20 minutes from King's Cross.

Best Times to Visit

Weekday lunchtimes offer a quieter experience at most venues, while weekends showcase Highgate's bustling village atmosphere. Sunday roasts at the gastropubs are extremely popular—book ahead or arrive early to secure a table.

Combine with Nearby Attractions

Make the most of your visit by exploring Highgate Cemetery (booking required), taking a walk on Hampstead Heath, or wandering through Waterlow Park and Highgate Woods. Many of the eateries mentioned above are perfect pit stops before or after outdoor activities.

Weather Tip

On sunny days, prioritize venues with outdoor spaces: The Bull's leafy terrace, The Red Lion & Sun's two beer gardens, or The Pavilion Cafe in Waterlow Park. Highgate's green spaces and outdoor dining options are among its greatest assets.

Price Ranges

Highgate caters to various budgets. Cafes and traditional pubs offer affordable options, while award-winning gastropubs like The Red Lion & Sun command premium prices reflecting their exceptional quality. Most venues fall into the £10-25 per person range for meals, with gastropub dining averaging £40-75 per person including drinks.

Final Thoughts

Highgate N6's food and drink scene perfectly reflects the neighborhood's character: historic yet contemporary, sophisticated yet welcoming, and always maintaining a distinct village charm despite its London location.

Whether you're seeking a centuries-old pub with literary connections, an award-winning gastropub serving exceptional modern British cuisine, or a peaceful cafe tucked into parkland, Highgate delivers authentic experiences that make the journey to North London worthwhile.

The combination of quality establishments, beautiful surroundings, and genuine neighborhood atmosphere ensures that eating and drinking in Highgate feels special—this isn't just about the food and drink, it's about the complete experience of one of London's most charming villages.
